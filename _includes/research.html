Simulation Of A Platform For Tracking Model Rockets In Flight

For investigations of rocket flights, usually flight data such as from an accelerometer is up to interpretation. While on-board cameras allow for a specific view point they are often limited by; field of view, structural requirements and avionics requirements. If the flight results in a catastrophic failure, data may be unrecoverable.

My capstone aims to lay the foundation simulation for developing a rocket tracking platform to track model rockets in flight. This will allow rocketeers to better analyse their rockets in flight. The goal being that building off this capstone a tracking platform can be built that is small enough for transport to launch sites and cost to build is low enough for university rocketeers to construct. To do this a simulation is built to fly low powered rockets, a computer vision system to detect the rocket, a control system to follow to rocket with a camera, the maximum speeds of which the camera must rotate to keep up with the rocket, and the required focal lengths are calculated and simulated.
